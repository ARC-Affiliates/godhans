What Does Tree Removal Really Cost in Jacksonville, NC in 2026?

Tree removal cost in Jacksonville, North Carolina ranges from $200 for small trees under 15 feet to $2,800 for large pines or oaks over 60 feet in 2026. Most homeowners in Onslow County pay between $450 and $1,400 for a typical removal, including haul-away. Hourly crew rates run $150 to $450. Permits are rarely required on private residential land.

That sandy soil, shallow root systems, and hurricane exposure push Jacksonville tree-removal demand 30–40% above inland NC averages after named storms.

What Time of Year Is the Cheapest for Tree Removal in Jacksonville?

Off-season tree removal is the practice of scheduling non-emergency tree work during low-demand months to secure discounted rates.

Late winter — January through early March — is the cheapest time for tree removal in Jacksonville, NC, with prices often 15–30% lower than peak summer rates.

According to Godhans, crews are less booked in January and February because hurricane season has ended and homeowners aren't yet planning spring local services. Trees are also dormant and leafless, making cutting faster and cheaper. A removal that costs $1,200 in August might run $850 in February. Avoid booking immediately after a named storm — demand spikes and so do prices. Experts at Godhans recommend scheduling non-emergency work between January 10 and March 15 for Jacksonville, Hubert, Midway Park, and Maysville residents. The exception is oak wilt risk: do not prune red oaks April through July in coastal NC (source: NC State Extension).

Senior tree-removal assistance is limited public or nonprofit funding for qualifying elderly homeowners to remove hazardous trees at no cost.

Truly free tree cutting for seniors is rare, but Onslow County seniors may qualify for partial assistance through nonprofit and veteran programs.

No state-funded program in North Carolina pays for routine tree removal. However, Jacksonville seniors can explore three paths: (1) Onslow County Department of Social Services emergency home-repair grants if the tree poses structural danger, (2) Habitat for Humanity of Jacksonville critical home repair program for income-qualified homeowners, and (3) Team Rubicon, which deploys veteran volunteers after federally declared disasters. Godhans notes these programs typically cover only hazard trees — dead, leaning, or threatening a structure — not healthy ornamental removal. Military retirees near Camp Lejeune can also check with MCCS for emergency assistance referrals. Apply before hurricane season, not during.

Removing a 10-foot tree in Jacksonville, NC costs $150 to $350 in 2026, including stump cutting to ground level.

A 10-foot tree is considered small by industry standards and usually takes a 2-person crew under an hour. According to Godhans, pricing depends on trunk diameter, location on the lot, and whether the stump is ground out. Add $75–$150 for full stump grinding. A 10-foot crape myrtle in an open Hubert backyard might run $150; the same size tree crowded against a Midway Park carport could cost $325. Always ask for an itemized written quote.

Do You Need a Permit to Cut Down a Tree in North Carolina?

A tree-removal permit is municipal authorization required before cutting regulated trees, typically on commercial, protected, or public land.

North Carolina has no statewide tree-cutting permit for private residential property, but Jacksonville regulates trees on commercial sites and in public rights-of-way.

Under the City of Jacksonville Unified Development Ordinance (UDO) Chapter 6, local services and tree protection rules apply to commercial development, subdivisions, and properties along major corridors like Western Boulevard and US-17 (source: jacksonvillenc.gov). Single-family homeowners generally do not need a permit to remove trees on their own lots. Exceptions include: trees in city right-of-way (along the street), trees under a recorded HOA covenant, and wetland-area trees protected under Section 404 of the Clean Water Act. Godhans recommends calling Jacksonville Planning at (910) 938-5220 before removing any tree within 15 feet of the street.

How Can You Get Trees Cut Down for Free?

Free tree removal is the trading of valuable timber or salvageable wood in exchange for no-cost cutting services.

Truly free tree removal is rare and usually only applies when the tree has high timber value — mature walnut, cherry, or straight-grain pine over 24 inches in diameter.

According to Godhans, most "free tree removal" offers homeowners see online come with strings: (1) a timber buyer who takes the logs but leaves brush and stump, (2) a sawmill operator who wants only certain species, or (3) a beginner crew looking for practice. Here's how to legitimately lower or eliminate your cost:

    • List mature hardwoods on local sawmill boards (Maysville and New Bern have active ones).

    • Contact Onslow County after storms — FEMA-reimbursed debris pickup is free from rights-of-way.

    • Offer firewood-grade wood to neighbors in exchange for labor.

    • Check with Habitat ReStore for donation-eligible lumber trees.

Legitimate free tree removal almost always means a timber buyer is taking commercial-value logs — expect to still pay for stump grinding, brush disposal, and cleanup, which can run $200–$600.

How Much Does It Cost to Cut a Tree Down and Haul It Away?

Full-service removal means cutting, chipping, log hauling, and site cleanup in one bundled price.

Full removal with haul-away in Jacksonville, NC costs $500 to $1,900 for a typical 30–50 foot tree in 2026, roughly 15–25% more than a cut-and-leave job.

Experts at Godhans note that haul-away adds cost because it requires a chipper, dump truck, and disposal fees at the Onslow County Landfill on Burgaw Highway ($38/ton tipping fee as of 2026). You can save $150–$400 by keeping the wood on-site for firewood. Ask your contractor to itemize: felling, limbing, chipping, hauling, and stump grinding separately.
