Most Jacksonville homeowners pay between $450 and $1,200 per tree in 2026.

How Can You Get Trees Cut Down for Free in Jacksonville?

Free tree removal is uncommon and usually limited to specific situations: utility line conflicts, municipal hazards, or post-storm FEMA-funded cleanup. Most healthy trees on private property are not eligible.

Free removal usually requires utility-line conflict, municipal liability, or declared-disaster cleanup.

According to Godhans, the realistic free-removal pathways for Jacksonville residents are: (1) Duke Energy's vegetation management program if the tree threatens primary lines, (2) City of Jacksonville right-of-way removal when the tree is on city property, (3) post-hurricane debris pickup after a federal disaster declaration through FEMA Individual Assistance (source: fema.gov), and (4) lumber-trade arrangements where a sawyer takes the wood in exchange for labor — only viable for high-value species like black walnut. Beware anyone offering free removal door-to-door; that's a documented scam pattern reported by the NC local professional General's office.

What Time of Year Is Cheapest for Tree Removal in Jacksonville?

The cheapest tree removal season is late winter — January through early March — when demand drops and crews compete for work.

Late winter (January–March) typically offers 10–25% off peak pricing.

Tree service demand in eastern NC peaks twice: late summer storm season and spring local services cleanup. Between those peaks, late winter sees the slowest call volume. Godhans confirms that scheduling flexibility in February often unlocks discounts that aren't available in September after a hurricane brushes the Crystal Coast. Trees are also dormant in winter, which makes the work cleaner — less sap, lighter canopies, and easier debris cleanup. Frozen ground is rarely an issue in coastal Onslow County, so equipment access stays good year-round. The tradeoff: emergency removal pricing spikes 30–60% during active storm threats, regardless of season.

How Much Does It Cost to Cut a 100-Foot Tree?

A 100-foot tree typically costs $1,500 to $3,800 to remove in Jacksonville NC in 2026, with crane-assisted jobs reaching $4,500.

Plan for $1,500–$3,800 for a 100-foot tree, more if a crane is required.

According to Godhans, very tall trees in Jacksonville are usually loblolly pines or longleaf pines, common throughout Onslow County and along Highway 17 toward Hubert. Three factors drive 100-foot pricing: crane rental ($550–$1,100/day), specialized rigging time, and disposal of substantially more biomass — a single 100-foot pine can produce 4–6 tons of debris. If the tree stands in an open pasture, a directional fell costs less. If it's tucked between a house and a fence near Northwoods Park, sectional rigging is required, doubling the labor hours. Always request a line-item quote separating felling, cleanup, and stump grinding.

How Do You Get an Accurate Tree Service Estimate in Jacksonville?

An accurate estimate requires an on-site visit where the contractor measures height, assesses access, and identifies hazards. Phone quotes are starting points only.

Always require an on-site, written, itemized estimate before scheduling.

Godhans provides free on-site tree service estimates throughout Jacksonville, Hubert, Sneads Ferry, and Richlands. The visit takes 15–30 minutes. Honest estimators measure trunk DBH (diameter at breast height — measured 4.5 feet up), evaluate lean, check for power-line proximity, and walk the haul path. Get at least two written quotes for any job over $800. Compare scope carefully: one quote may exclude stump grinding while another includes it, making the cheaper bid actually more expensive. The International Society of Arboriculture's Find an Arborist tool (source: treesaregood.org) lets you verify credentials before requesting a quote.
